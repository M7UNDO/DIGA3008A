Blog 1: Reflecting on the Web and My Digital Journey

My chosen reading, The Library of Babel imagined a universe containing every book in the world, whether meaningful or not. Today, that universe fits in my pocket; my smartphone gives me access to infinite knowledge at the click of a button. The search for truth in Borges' The Library of Babel mirrors how I interact with the internet on a daily basis. However, there is one key difference. Instead of wandering through endless hexagonal rooms, I simply tap on a screen, and my phone connects me to nearly all recorded human knowledge. Whether I'm Googling for academic sources, checking and scrolling social media, or using AI for quick summaries, my phone functions as my own personal library. Today we are constantly bombarded with information, but in Borges' time, seeking knowledge was a slow and painstaking process (Borges, 2013).

Blog 2: Moulthrop, Hypertext, and Designing with Structure

Moulthrop's work remains a relevant critique of today's internet. Through his exploration of hypertext, he questioned how it would potentially change society and the media as we knew it. Moulthrop argued that while hypertext promised decentralisation and interaction, the reality would be shaped by corporate dominance and traditional models of broadcasting. As I consider how much the internet has developed, his insights seem almost prophetic; it is evident that concerns about corporate control and mass consumption have come true, as what was initially envisioned as an open, user-driven space has become largely controlled by major platforms like Google and Facebook (Meta), much like how television networks centralised media consumption before.

Blog 3:User Alignment, Stucture and Semantic Mark-up

My goal for this website is to showcase my game development and art projects in an engaging and intuitive way that allows my potential employers and fellow developers to easily navigate through my website and understand who I am, my skills, and my experience. This means I need to ensure that my site is easily accessible, clear, and well-structured, with a good interaction flow that allows users to seamlessly explore my work. I also want my interface to immediately communicate the purpose of my website so users can learn more about me and get in touch if they are interested. My target audience is people within the game and web development industries who are on the website to engage with my past projects and learn about me in order to consider me for professional positions.
