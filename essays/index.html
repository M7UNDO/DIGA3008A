Essay 1: UX & UI Analysis

In today's digital world, the quality of a website's User Interface (UI) and User Experience (UX) play a crucial role in the development of digital products, as it directly impacts how users interact and engage with them (Hamidli, 2023: 1-3 ). UI refers to the visual and interactive elements that users engage with, such as buttons, icons, and the layout, while UX encompasses the broader emotional and cognitive experience of using the digital system. These design elements go beyond the functionality and aesthetics, as they operate as signs and signifiers, forming a semiotic system that communicates meaning to the user.

UI and UX do not only guide the user's actions, but they also influence their interpretation, trust and perception. Thus, UI and UX are systems of communication, and interaction design becomes a language users must read and respond to. Well-designed UI can make a digital product more aesthetically pleasing, intuitive and simple to use, while well-designed UX can ensure a more positive experience throughout the user's journey on the website. Successful implementation of UI and UX design can directly increase user satisfaction, loyalty, and engagement with the digital product (Hamidli, 2023: 1-3 ).

Ghanchi notes that the design of a system carries an ethical weight. This means that any product that is created embodies a value system, clearly indicating the designer's beliefs and moral principles. Poor or manipulative design can exclude, confuse, or even exploit users, while good, ethical design can empower them by being inclusive, accessible and transparent with users (Ghanchi: 2021). This is especially vital when the digital platform serves a vulnerable user base, such as the National Student Financial Aid Scheme (NSFAS). NSFAS was created to offer financial assistance to disadvantaged South African students for their tertiary education.
